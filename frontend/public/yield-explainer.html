Where does the yield come from?

A plain-English breakdown of how leveraged supply APY is calculated, what can go wrong, and how liquidation works.

1. Base yield: supply interest

When you deposit an asset into a Blend pool, you earn interest paid by borrowers. The interest rate follows a 3-kink utilisation curve — the more of the pool that is borrowed, the higher the rate.

Supply APR = BorrowRate × (1 − BackstopTake) × Utilisation
// BackstopTake ≈ 20% — goes to the Blend backstop insurance fund
// Utilisation = totalBorrow / totalSupply

The borrow rate itself is determined by which segment of the curve the pool is in:

// Segment 1: util ≤ target (e.g. 80%)
BorrowRate = r_base + r_one × (util / target)
// Segment 2: target < util ≤ 95%
BorrowRate = r_base + r_one + r_two × slope
// Segment 3: util > 95% — steep cliff
BorrowRate = r_base + r_one + r_two + r_three × slope

This is the exact model in blend.ts::fetchAllReserves and projectRates. The UI re-runs it with your projected deposit to show how your position will affect rates.

2. BLND emissions

Blend distributes its governance token (BLND) to both suppliers and borrowers at a fixed rate per second (eps — emissions per second). This is an additional yield on top of interest.

BLND Supply APR = (eps × seconds_per_year × BLND_price) / (totalSupply × asset_price) × 100

Key properties of BLND emissions:

  • They are linear, not compounding. The UI shows them as APR, not APY.
  • They dilute as the pool grows. More depositors = smaller share per depositor.
  • BLND price is volatile. If BLND falls, this component of yield falls with it.
  • You must claim them manually — use "Claim" or "Claim & Convert" to realise them.
⚠ BLND emissions are not guaranteed. The Blend governance can change emission rates. Do not rely on BLND APR as the primary source of yield.

3. Borrow cost

To lever up, Turbolong borrows the same asset you deposited. You pay the borrow interest rate on the borrowed portion. Borrowers also receive BLND emissions, which partially offset this cost.

Net Borrow Cost = BorrowInterestAPR − BLND Borrow APR

At high utilisation (above 95%), borrow rates spike sharply. This is intentional — it protects the pool from running out of liquidity. If you open a large position near the utilisation cap, your borrow cost can be significantly higher than the displayed rate.

4. How leverage amplifies (and risks)

At leverage L on an initial deposit of E:

Total supply = E × L
Total borrow = E × (L − 1)
Equity = E // unchanged

Leverage multiplies your supply yield — but also your borrow cost. The net effect on your equity:

Gross yield on equity = NetSupplyAPR × L
Borrow cost on equity = NetBorrowCost × (L − 1)
Net APR on equity = NetSupplyAPR × L − NetBorrowCost × (L − 1)

This is the exact formula used in updatePreview() and renderPosition() in the UI.

LeverageSupply yieldBorrow costNet APR on equity
1×+5.0%—+5.0%
3×+15.0%−8.0%+7.0%
5×+25.0%−16.0%+9.0%
8×+40.0%−28.0%+12.0%
8× (rates spike)+40.0%−45.0%−5.0%

The last row illustrates the key risk: if borrow rates rise above supply rates, the position bleeds equity every day.

5. The net APY formula

The UI converts APR to APY for interest (which compounds) but keeps BLND as APR (which does not auto-compound). The displayed "Net APY" is therefore an approximation:

Net APY ≈ APR_to_APY(interestSupplyAPR) × L
− APR_to_APY(interestBorrowAPR) × (L − 1)
+ blndSupplyAPR × L
− blndBorrowAPR × (L − 1)
// APR_to_APY(r) = (e^(r/100) − 1) × 100

The tooltip on every APY figure in the app shows the underlying APR for comparison.

Note: Blend does not auto-compound. Interest accrues continuously on-chain, but your bToken balance only grows when you interact with the pool. The APY figure assumes continuous compounding — actual returns will be slightly lower unless you actively reinvest.

6. Worst-case scenarios

Borrow rate spike

If pool utilisation exceeds 95%, borrow rates enter the steep third segment and can reach 100%+ APR. This can flip a profitable position negative within days. The "Days until liquidation" counter in the UI estimates this using the current interest spread.

BLND price collapse

If BLND falls to near zero, the BLND APR component disappears. At high leverage, this can make the net APR negative even if interest rates are unchanged.

Asset price drop

Turbolong uses recursive lending on a single asset — you supply and borrow the same token. This means you have no directional price exposure to that asset. However, if the oracle price drops, your Health Factor can still fall because the pool values your collateral at the new lower price.

Oracle failure / exploit

Blend relies on on-chain oracles. A manipulated or stale price can cause incorrect liquidations. The Etherfuse pool was frozen in February 2026 following an oracle-related exploit — this is a real, non-theoretical risk.

⚠ You can lose your entire deposit. Leveraged positions can be liquidated faster than you can react, especially during periods of high volatility or network congestion. Never deposit more than you can afford to lose.

7. Liquidation mechanics

Blend liquidates positions when the Health Factor (HF) drops to or below 1.0:

HF = (collateral × c_factor) / (debt / l_factor)
// c_factor: collateral discount (e.g. 0.95 = 95%)
// l_factor: liability factor (e.g. 1.0 = no discount on debt)
// HF < 1.0 → liquidatable

HF decays over time because borrow interest accrues faster than supply interest (the spread). The rate of decay:

Days to liquidation ≈ ln(HF) / (borrowAPR − supplyAPR) × 365
// This is the formula used in the "Days until liquidation" metric

When a position is liquidated:

  1. A liquidator repays some or all of your debt.
  2. They receive your collateral at a discount (the liquidation bonus).
  3. You keep whatever collateral remains after the liquidator is paid — which may be very little at high leverage.

How to protect yourself

  • Keep HF above 1.1 — the UI warns at 1.01 but the safe zone is higher.
  • Claim & Convert BLND regularly — reinvesting emissions extends your runway.
  • Use Resupply — depositing extra collateral raises HF without closing the position.
  • Watch the "Days until liquidation" counter — if it drops below 30 days, act.
  • Set APY alerts — subscribe to email alerts when net APY turns negative.
There is no stop-loss. Blend does not support conditional orders. If you are not monitoring your position, it can be liquidated without warning.
